438-558. Aeneas is now attracted by the noise of the combatants to the

an¹ entrance by a private postern gate, and ascending to the roof, aids the

the assailants. From the roof he sees the Greeks under Pyrrhus finally burst
through the principal gate, and rush into the interior courts. He sees'Pyr-
